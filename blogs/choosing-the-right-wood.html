The Foundation of Quality: Wood Selection

The choice of wood is paramount in custom cabinet making. It influences not only the cabinet's appearance and style but also its durability, longevity, and cost. At Jerry's Cabinets, we guide our clients through the selection process, ensuring the chosen material perfectly aligns with their needs and aesthetic preferences.

Popular Wood Choices and Their Characteristics:

  • Oak: Known for its strength and prominent grain, oak is a classic choice. It's very durable and takes stain well, available in red oak (with a warmer, pinkish hue) and white oak (more greyish-brown).
  • Maple: A hard, dense wood with a fine, subtle grain. Maple is very durable and offers a smooth, clean look, making it ideal for contemporary designs or painted finishes.
  • Cherry: Prized for its rich color that deepens with age, cherry wood has a fine, straight grain. It's a moderately hard wood that gives a luxurious, warm feel.
  • Walnut: A strong, stable wood with a beautiful, rich chocolate brown color and varied grain patterns. Walnut adds a touch of elegance and sophistication.
  • Hickory: One of the hardest and strongest domestic woods, hickory is known for its distinctive grain and color variations, offering a rustic or robust look.

Beyond these, there are many other options, including engineered woods for specific applications. Understanding the properties of each can help you make an informed decision for your custom cabinetry project.
